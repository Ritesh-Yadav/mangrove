Public Health

Family Planning and Reproductive Health

In 2010, Marie Stopes Madagascar (MSM) began collecting and storing information sent in via mobile phone from doctors affiliated with its "BlueStar" network of medical clinics.

Using simple SMS technology, doctors in the network send in data regarding Family Planning services performed on a monthly basis.

All data is aggregated in a central database, accessible to headquarters staff, where it can be verified, filtered and exported for analysis.

Maternal and Child Health

In 2010, the USAID-funded RTI/Santénet2 Project adopted a mobile phone reporting system to collect health services data delivered by the project.

Using HNI’s data collection and communication platform, RTI/Santénet2 has collected reliable information regarding

  • Maternal health and family planning
  • Child malnutrition
  • Diarrhea, respiratory infection and malaria

Humanitarian Component, Madagascar

Risk and Disaster Management

In 2010, the National Bureau for Risk and Disaster Management (known by the French acronym BNGRC) launched a new initiative to collect data electronically after a natural disaster.

Using SMS, local officials now send in their first post-cyclone data from the affected areas to headquarters. Managers in the capital, Antananarivo, can view the data in spreadsheets and maps and then make more informed decisions.
