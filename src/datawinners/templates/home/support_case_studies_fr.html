MONTHLY ACTIVITY REPORTS

1. Replace Costly and Inefficient Paper-Based Data Collection Efforts

Osi has a problem: As program manager of a large child malnutrition project, she cannot get timely, accurate and complete data from Nutritional Agents at each of her Feeding Centers.

2. Upgrade to Data Collection via SMS

Osi decides to collect her data via mobile phone. She reviews her paper-based monthly summary report form, talks with staff and decides: 1) who she wants to collect data from (Nutritional Agents); 2) The subject of her questionnaire (Feeding Centers) and 3) which key questions she wants her Nutritional Agents to answer via SMS (example: How many children did you weigh last month?)

She creates the questionnaire using DataWinners friendly questionnaire builder.

3. Create Training Materials to Teach Staff How to Send in Data Via SMS

DataWinners creates for Osi a template of her Questionnaire. Osi uses the template to train her Nutritional Agents to send in monthly summary reports via SMS.

4. Use DataWinners to send reminders

Osi sets an automatic reminder to be sent to all Nutritional Agents who haven’t yet submitted their report.

5. View your Data from DataWinners website

The day after the submission deadline, Osi logs in to her DataWinners account and sees complete monthly reports from all 100 Feeding Centers. For the first time, Osi has all her data, on time.

6. Share Your Data

Osi provides her supervisor with a link to the monthly summary data.

7. Make better decisions

Her supervisor is thrilled to have the information. Osi and the supervisor review the data and decide to make changes at several Feeding Centers. Osi congratulates many of her Nutritional Agents both for sending in their information and for meeting their project goals.

DETAILED RECORDS

1. Collect Multiple Reports Simultaneously

In addition to Monthly Summary Reports, Osi also wants her Nutritional Agents to send in key data after each child’s visit to a Feeding Center.

2. Improve the quality of your data

Osi creates a second data collection project in her DataWinners account. She reviews her paper-based Feeding Center Visit Report form, talks with staff and decides: 1) who she wants to collect data from (Nutritional Agents); 2) The subject of her questionnaire (Each Child who visits) and 3) which key questions she wants her Nutritional Agents to answer via SMS (example: How much does the child weigh in kilograms?)

She creates the questionnaire using DataWinners friendly questionnaire builder.

3. Register Each Child in DataWinners

Osi uses her DataWinners account to create a simple registration form for each child who visits a feeding center:

  1. What is the child's first name?
  2. What is the child's last name?
  3. Child's date of birth?
  4. Mother's telephone number?

Once the Nutrional Agents send in the answers to these registration questions, DataWinners generates a unique identification number for each child.

4. Create Training Materials that Teach Staff How to Send in Data Via SMS

DataWinners creates for Osi a template of her Questionnaire. Osi uses the template to train her Nutritional Agents to send in the feeding center visit reports via SMS.

5. View Data from DataWinners Website

On a daily basis, Osi can review the feeding center visit reports in real time from all 100 of her Feeding Centers. Osi can identify each severely malnourished child and work with Nutritional Agent on a recovery plan

6. Compare Results over Time

Using DataWinners data analysis tools, Osi can track the progress of each child over time.

SUPERVISOR REPORTS

1. Quality Assurance Data

Osi wants to collect quality assurance data from her Regional Supervisors after each visit to a Feeding Center.

2. Create a New Project in DataWinners

Osi creates a third data collection project in her DataWinners account. She reviews her paper-based Feeding Center Quality Assurance Report form, talks with staff and decides: 1) who she wants to collect data from (Regional Supervisors); 2) The subject of her questionnaire (Feeding Center) and 3) which key questions she wants her Supervisors to answer via SMS (example: What overall grade, out of five do you give this Feeding Center?)

She creates the questionnaire using DataWinners friendly questionnaire builder.

3. Register Each Feeding Center in DataWinners

Osi uses her DataWinners account to create a simple registration form for each feeding center:

  • What is name of Feeding Center?
  • Where is Feeding Center located?

Once Osi answers these questions, DataWinners generates a unique identification number for each Feeding Center

4. Create Training Materials that Teach Staff How to Send in Data Via SMS

DataWinners creates for Osi a template of her Questionnaire. Osi uses the template to train her Supervisors to send in the feeding center visit reports via SMS.

5. View Data from DataWinners Website

On a daily basis, Osi can review the Feeding Center Quality Assurance reports in real time. Osi can identify which Feeding Centers require immediate assistance.

6. Compare Results over Time

Using DataWinners data analysis tools, Osi can track the progress of each Feeding Center over time.
