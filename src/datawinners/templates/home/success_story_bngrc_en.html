About the Projet

In the event of natural or anthropogenic disaster, the National Bureau for Risk and Disaster Management (BNGRC) is the entity responsible for coordinating programs and activities linked to emergency response and assistance. For some hazards, the bureau is also responsible for the preparedness and prevention to reduce the impact of disasters.

The Challenge

For several years, post-hazard data have been collected through either

  • direct landline phone
  • radio communication (SSB), or
  • hard copy version by the means of cards, for communities close to the BNGRC

Obtaining the cards from the affected communities requires a fairly long time to reach the Commune and the District level, and then the BNGRC. Moreover, the processing of such information requires about 30 minutes for each card collected. Such information processing was heavy, and the cost of telephone calls increased in parallel with the extent of the damages.

« Every year, an estimated average of 500 villages are affected by cyclones, which are the major hazards. The processing of information is time consuming, involves a lot of personnel, and delays the decision-making and the sending of response teams; however, in the meantime, the extent of the damages is increasing. It is even possible that there will be deaths before the arrival of the emergency response team », accroding to Nianja Raonivelo, BNGRC Webmaster/Data Analyst.

Action Taken

In collaboration with the members of CRIC and SALOHI project, BNGRC decided to take advantage of the use of HNI’s SMS technology for rapid reporting and processing of post-hazard data. The goal is to provide more alternatives to the affected communities for the sending of the post-flood and post-cyclone information, and to reduce the processing time of information that reach the BNGRC. 1700 chips have been purchased by BNGRC.
The United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) will proceed with the purchase of 300 telephone sets. International partners such as CARE, CRS, ADRA and UNDP supported the on-the-spot in chain training, which is currently underway in some areas vulnerable to flooding and cyclone. The most relevant indicators have been identified and HNI has developed an application for the data processing.

The Result

BNGRC is currently in the phase of training village Leaders for the sending of data by SMS.
According to Nianja Raonivelo, « Now, the communication, the processing and the compilation of data can be done quickly. We can do the mapping, send messages to raise pre-hazard awareness and preparedness. Information can be shared online and on time because they are available on our website. "
The system was considered affordable, relevant and reliable ».

Le système a été considéré comme abordable, intéressant et fiable.
